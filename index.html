Symposium

CCMF 2026 - The 2nd International Symposium for Young Researchers - Challenges & Collaboration in the Research of Material Fracture - Civil Engineering & Construction

March 12–14, 2026 • The University of Osaka • Nakanoshima Center

Aim

CCMF 2026 brings together young researchers and early-career professionals at the interface of civil engineering, construction materials, and structural fracture and deterioration. The symposium concentrates on macro to meso scale behavior that governs the performance of real infrastructure, including bridges, buildings, offshore platforms, pipelines, and urban systems. Core themes include welding and joining for infrastructure; structural performance and fatigue; durability management with emphasis on corrosion protection, protective coatings, and repair and retrofit technologies; structural health monitoring and nondestructive evaluation; and life-cycle reliability, risk, and decision making. The program encourages contributions that connect field measurements, laboratory testing, and high-fidelity modeling, and that leverage data-driven approaches, digital twins, and machine learning for prognosis, inspection planning, and asset management. Through open exchange, mentorship, and collaboration across disciplines and institutions, CCMF 2026 seeks to translate research into practice and to accelerate solutions that improve the safety, resilience, and sustainability of civil infrastructure. Submissions may include experimental studies, field case studies, numerical and probabilistic analyses, code and guideline development, and life-cycle assessment.

Scope

Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

Fracture & fatigue in civil structures

  • Steel, concrete, and composite systems
  • Orthotropic decks; variable-amplitude and traffic loading
  • Corrosion-fatigue scenarios in service
  • Fracture mechanics for structural components and details

Welding & joining for infrastructure

  • Weld design and quality; residual stress and distortion
  • Post-weld improvement: peening, heat treatments, surface modification
  • Repair & retrofit strategies including friction-based processes

Durability & maintenance

  • Corrosion mechanisms: marine, deicing salts, industrial atmospheres
  • Protective coatings, galvanizing/metalizing, cathodic protection
  • Sealing systems, weathering steel performance, service-life extension

Inspection, SHM & NDE

  • Field measurements; ultrasonic, AE, guided-wave, eddy-current methods
  • Fiber-optic sensing; UAV/vision-based inspection
  • Data fusion and digital twins for condition assessment and prognosis

Modeling & data

  • FE/XFEM/cohesive-zone and peridynamics for structural fracture
  • Reliability and risk; stochastic degradation
  • Machine learning for prognosis and decision support
  • Bayesian updating with monitoring data

Environment & extreme actions

  • Seismic loading, wind/typhoons, temperature and humidity effects
  • Hydrogen or other aggressive media
  • Climate resilience and adaptation for long-span and coastal structures

Codes, practice & management

  • Performance-based design
  • Inspection and maintenance planning
  • Life-cycle cost and sustainability
  • Circular use and repurposing of structural members
  • Case studies from field applications

CCMF 2026 particularly welcomes cross-disciplinary contributions that link materials engineering with structural design, assessment, and asset management, as well as collaboration-oriented sessions for young researchers.
